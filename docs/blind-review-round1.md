# Blind music review — round 1

## Why this round exists

Automation has already confirmed that all candidates decode, contain finite
samples, avoid clipping, and have immutable generation evidence. Automation
cannot decide whether music is distracting, supports the named activity, or is
pleasant enough for repeated use.

Round 1 therefore contains only eight blinded candidates: two technically
promising options for each activity that does not yet have accepted music. The
other eight candidates remain quarantined. This shortlist is prioritisation,
not approval or rejection.

## Listening load

- Review one activity at a time.
- Listen during a real matching task, not as foreground entertainment.
- Use the same comfortable master volume for both candidates.
- Give each candidate at least 10 minutes when tolerable.
- Stop immediately for speech, a notification-like sound, discomfort, or a
  strongly attention-capturing event.
- Two candidates per day is enough. There is no benefit in rushing.

## Round 1 order

| Activity | Blind candidates | Suggested real task |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Learning | I, J | Read and summarise a moderately difficult article |
| Creativity | O, N | Draft, sketch, or brainstorm without editing |
| Motivation | Q, R | Begin a task that has been easy to postpone |
| Light Work | U, X | Email, filing, or routine administration |

The order within each pair should be alternated on a later session if both seem
acceptable. Do not inspect filenames, genre mappings, prompts, or technical
scores while rating.

## Three decisions per candidate

Record only these after the task:

1. **Distraction:** none, noticeable but workable, or task-breaking.
2. **Activity fit:** poor, acceptable, or strong.
3. **Repeat willingness:** no, maybe, or yes.

Also flag any specific event: speech/voice, notification-like tone, foreground
melody, build/drop, abrupt transition, harshness, or obvious short-loop fatigue.

## Advancement rule

A candidate advances to a representative-work-session check only when:

- distraction is `none` or `noticeable but workable`;
- activity fit is `acceptable` or `strong`;
- repeat willingness is `maybe` or `yes`;
- there is no speech, notification-like event, abrupt transition, or discomfort.

If neither candidate for an activity advances, review the held-back pair for
that activity. No candidate receives publication approval from this round.

## Held-back candidates

| Activity | Blind candidates | Reason for later review |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Learning | K, L | More note/event activity |
| Creativity | M, P | Greater rhythmic density; P also needs transition scrutiny |
| Motivation | S, T | Larger dynamics or reduced transition margin |
| Light Work | V, W | Higher event density or more foreground/loudness risk |

## Evidence boundary

This procedure does not test or claim treatment of ADHD. It tests personal
task compatibility and listening quality for a productivity-audio product.
